Opening Angle

Climate activists are increasingly targeting pension funds and endowments through shareholder engagement and divestment campaigns.These campaigns often focus on fossil fuel divestment and sustainable investment integration. These campaigns leverage fiduciary duty arguments to push for fossil fuel divestment and sustainable investment practices. Understanding their tactics and having prepared responses helps maintain productive dialogue while protecting investment autonomy.

Recommended Approach

  • Lead with: Acknowledge shared concern for long-term value creation and beneficiary outcomes
  • Implementation: Present existing sustainable investment integration processes and climate risk assessment frameworks already in place
  • Reporting: Provide clear metrics on sustainability performance and climate-related financial disclosures
  • Risk discipline: Emphasize evidence-based investment decisions that balance all material risks including climate, regulatory, and transition risks

Screening Intelligence

We employ rigorous exclusionary screens for product-based violations (controversial weapons, tobacco) and conduct-based violations (forced labor, UN Global Compact breaches). Our screening process involves both negative screens and positive tilts toward companies demonstrating strong sustainability performance. This approach balances values alignment with financial objectives while maintaining portfolio diversification.
